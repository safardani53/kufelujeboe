Celebrity Weddings of 2022 [PHOTOS]

Bubba Wallace & Amanda Carter

Bubba Wallace and Amanda Carter entered the new year as husband and wife!

The NASCAR driver and Carter exchanged vows in front of 250 guests at the JW Marriott in Charlotte, North Carolina, on Dec. 31.

"We wanted the ceremony, (which was held on the hotel's 4th floor) to be formal, and then as soon as you walk down the staircase to the reception, we wanted the whole mood to flip," Carter told PEOPLE. "The flowers are changing from white to vibrant with pinks, purples, reds, with a lot of sparkle and shine in the room. We wanted the reception to feel like a New Year's Eve party."

The bride wore a gown by Madam Burcu Couture paired with shoes by Lola Cruz for her walk down the aisle. Wallace chose a custom dark red velvet tuxedo designed by Stitched by Mitch.

Rather than a seated dinner, the couple opted for four different tapas-style stations and following a New Year's Eve champagne toast at 1 a.m., guests enjoyed chicken nuggets and French fries from a McDonald's food truck.

Daniella Monet & Andrew Gardner

After a five-year engagement — and more than a decade after they started dating — Daniella Monet and Andrew Gardner became husband and wife!

The Victorious alumna shared the big news in a series of Instagram posts. Posting three black-and-white photos, Monet captioned them "12," 29" and "22" — signifying her Dec. 29 wedding date.

The couple became parents to son Gio James, 3, in 2019, and then welcomed daughter Ivry, 22 months, in 2021.

Tiffany Maher & Kyle Spence

So You Think You Can Dance and Hit the Floor alum Tiffany Maher married entrepreneur Kyle Spence at the Bahia Mar resort by Hilton off the coast of Ft. Lauderdale, Florida, on Dec. 17 surrounded by 250 of their closest friends and family members.

The groom wore a traditional black tux from Indochino which matched Maher's traditional wedding look that included an Elysee dress.

"It wasn't the typical, you try on the first dress and say yes to the dress. I definitely went to a ton of stores," she told PEOPLE. "I ended up getting it from this place in Miami. I wore my hair down, just so that when I look back, it's me. I didn't do anything for the first time that day."

Makenzie Dustman & Phillip Chbeeb

So You Think You Can Dance alums Makenzie Dustman and Phillip Chbeeb are married!

Makenzie, who was featured on season 10 of the series, shared a carousel of pictures and videos from her nuptials to Phillip, who appeared on season 5, in a ceremony that took place at Lake Arrowhead, California.

"12.12.22. The world stopped for a moment… & life began again…," she wrote in the Instagram caption. "Introducing to you, officially, the Chbeeb's🥹🫀🫁✨🫂❄️."

Two days prior to the wedding, Makenzie said she and Phillip "married one another in a Melkite Catholic Church 10 mins from our house," according to a caption she wrote over an image from the ceremony on her Instagram Story.

"Phillip's papa bear was raised Melkite in Lebanon, and he and his parents and so on, were all married under this same religion, through the same intentional, sacred ceremony" she explained. "For us to be able to follow in his footsteps, was the most powerful, emotional thing. Our parents and two best friends were by our sides as we did it."

Mallory Pugh & Dansby Swanson

United States Women's National soccer team star Mallory Pugh married Atlanta Braves shortstop Dansby Swanson on Dec. 10 at the Ritz Carlton Reynolds Lake Oconee in Greensboro, Georgia.

"To get married in front of our family and friends is so special," the couple told PEOPLE exclusively. "It means that we are becoming one and get to impact God's kingdom together."

The bride wore a custom Anne Barge gown for her walk down the aisle and following the newlyweds' nuptials they changed into custom Nike's for dancing made by the Shoe Surgeon.

Seinne Fleming & Doug Fillmore

Bachelor alum Seinne Fleming and Doug Fillmore tied the knot in an intimate ceremony at the Cap Juluca, A Belmond Hotel in Anguilla.

"We are overjoyed to announce that we are officially married!" the couple captioned a joint Instagram announcement on Dec. 16. "Last week, we exchanged vows on the most breathtaking beach in Anguilla, an unforgettable trip marking the start of our journey together as husband and wife."

The two shared that though the beach nuptials were just for the newlyweds, they legally wed in front of friends and family in Los Angeles the month before.

"While this ceremony was just the two of us, we were blessed to have our families present during an intimate wedding in Los Angeles last month," the caption continued. We look forward to sharing moments from that special day very soon."

Porsha Williams & Simon Guobadia

Over Thanksgiving weekend, Real Housewives of Atlanta alumna, Porsha Williams, married Nigerian-born businessman, Simon Guobadia.

The two first wed wed in a Nigerian traditional native law and custom ceremony at the Four Seasons Hotel Atlanta, followed by a second American ceremony one day later at a local Methodist church with an elaborate reception at the St. Regis Atlanta.

During the weekend of festivities,Williams wore a total of seven different gowns between her two ceremonies and reception. "I just couldn't turn any dresses down," she told PEOPLE. "If a wedding dress came my way, and I loved it, I got it."

Guobadia was styled for both events by Jeremiah Ogbodo, who goes by Swanky Jerry, curating the groom's traditional Edo attire for the Nigerian ceremony. He also styled all three of Guobadia's looks for the American wedding.

One detail from the couple's stunning reception was a golden wedding cake that was so tall they needed to use a sword to cut it.

Naomi Biden & Peter Neal

President Joe Biden's granddaughter, Naomi Biden, wed Peter Neal in a long-anticipated White House wedding on Nov. 19.

The event was the first nuptials to take place at the White House in nearly a decade — and the first-ever for a grandchild of a sitting president.

The couple married in a private ceremony on the South Lawn which was followed immediately by a luncheon before a black-tie reception later that day.

Naomi chose a custom Ralph Lauren the gown for the ceremony made of Chantilly lace, featuring hand-placed organza petals along the bodice and cascading into the skirt. She paired the dress with a cathedral-length veil made of silk organza with a custom Chantilly lace border and embroidered detailing and Tiffany & Co. jewels. Neal also wore Ralph Lauren.

For the evening festivities, Naomi changed into a strapless Reem Acra dress with her grandmother Roberta Buhle's pearls sewn into the train.

Tiffany Trump & Michael Boulos

Tiffany Trump, daughter of former President Donald Trump and actress Marla Maples, married Michael Boulos on Nov. 12 at the Mar-a-Lago Club.

"We are focused on this sacred union and welcoming beloved friends and family, not on politics," Maples told PEOPLE of her daughter's wedding, adding that the family chose the venue because "this was Tiffany's childhood home and where she was brought into the world."

The newlyweds, who met at Lindsay Lohan's club in Mykonos, Greece, began dating in 2018 and got engaged in January 2021.

Monica Puig & Nathan Rakitt

Tennis player and Olympic gold medalist Monica Puig and longtime love Nathan Rakitt married on Nov. 11 at the Caribe Hilton Hotel in Puig's native Puerto Rico.

For the oceanfront nuptials, Puig's dress held special significance for the bride.

"For me it was super important to continue to embrace the island and I wanted someone from my island to make my dress," she said of the Harry Robles designed dress. "The inspiration was based off the ocean waves transitioning to the sand. The elements of the dress which is the beading to represent the seafoam and the fringe going in ombré from white to beige represents the transition of the crashing wave into the sand."

The couple also pulled from both of their backgrounds when choosing traditions to honor during the ceremony.

"My favorite part of the ceremony was smashing the glass after we were pronounced husband and wife," Puig said. "I'm Catholic and Nathan is Jewish, and we wanted to incorporate some traditions from both."

Taylor Launter & Taylor Dome

Taylor Lautner wed longtime girlfriend Taylor Dome on Nov. 11 at Epoch Estate Wines in California's wine country in front of around 90 friends and family.

"Everything felt so surreal," Dome told PEOPLE. "The flowers, the vines, the sky. We were both very involved in the process so we had high expectations, but it exceeded them for sure."

Dome, a registered nurse, wore a Winnie Couture gown as she walked down the aisle and Lautner sported a Dolce & Gabbana suit as the pair exchanged handwritten vows and bands by Ring Concierge.

Dome and Lautner both had second looks on deck.

Dome went with a "Retrofete asymmetrical lace-up sequined gown" while the Twilight star swapped his suit for a a short-sleeve, black velvet two-piece.

Michael Oher & Tiffany Roy

Retired professional football player Michael Oher, whose life inspired The Blind Side, married longtime love Tiffany Roy at the JW Marriot in Nashville, Tennessee, on Nov. 5.

The couple's son Kobi (they are also parents to son MJ and daughters Kierstin and Naivi) escorted Roy down the aisle to Beyonce's "Halo" where she joined Oher and recited personalized vows after their unity sand ceremony.

The couple, who had been together for 17 years, were joined by more than 200 guests for the celebration that included 8-foot-tall florals and crystals, ballerinas and a second line band — a nod to Roy's hometown of New Orleans.

Thommy Ten & Amélie van Tass

Austrian magician and mentalist duo, Thommy Ten and Amélie van Tass, tied the knot at A Little White Wedding Chapel in Las Vegas on Oct. 30.

The Clairvoyants, who were runner-ups on season 11 of America's Got Talent, told PEOPLE they kept the news of the Las Vegas wedding to themselves.

"It was just the two of us, our little dog Mr. Koni Hundini and a very good friend who was the witness," they continued. "After the wedding, we flew to Austria, where our family and friends live and told everyone about what had just happened. It felt like a honeymoon in our home country! Everyone was so happy and celebrated with us."

Following the ceremony at the famous venue, the newlyweds drove to downtown Las Vegas where they had pizza and took photos on historic Fremont Street before dining at Spago by Wolfgang Puck for dinner.

Astrid Loch & Kevin Wendt

Following a whirlwind romance that began on Bachelor in Paradise in 2018, Astrid Loch and Kevin Wendt are now officially husband and wife!

The couple married on Oct. 28 at the Powel Crosley Estate in Sarasota, Florida.

The big day came comes after the pair — who briefly split but later reconciled and got engaged in August 2019 — postponed their wedding due to the COVID-19 pandemic and welcomed their son, August, in November 2021.

Their son was at the alter with dad as Loch walked down the aisle to "Conversations in the Dark" by John Legend. The little one and Wendt wore a matching off-the-rack State & Liberty tuxedos. Loch dressed in a custom-made Gaila Lahav gown.

Serena Pitt & Joe Amabile

Serena Pitt and Joe Amabile, who got engaged on season 7 of Bachelor in Paradise, celebrated their love with with a city hall wedding on Oct. 27 in New York City.

"We still plan on having a big wedding next fall that we are already planning, but with everything being so public in our relationship, having a private moment just the two of us felt so special and intimate," the couple told PEOPLE exclusively at the time. "Natasha Parker was our witness, someone that has seen our relationship from the very beginning."

"We can't wait to celebrate with all of our family and friends next year at our wedding," they continued, "because who doesn't want to marry the love of their life twice?"

Brooke Eden & Hilary Hoover

Country artist Brooke Eden and Hilary Hoover celebrated their marriage with not one — but two — ceremonies!

On Aug. 27, the couple first wed in a legal ceremony held at a little white church in Nashville surrounded by their family.

Garth Brooks, who was Hoover's boss up until recently when she left to manage Eden's touring, was also in attendance. Brooks sang "To Make You Feel My Love" as Eden and Hoover walked down the aisle with their parents. His wife, Trisha Yearwood, served as the wedding officiant.

Two months later, on Oct. 27, the couple had 140 family and friends attend their second wedding ceremony at the Paradisus Playa del Carmen resort in Playa del Carmen, Mexico.

Country stars Mitchell Tenpenny and Meghan Patrick tied the knot on Oct. 23 in Goodlettsville, Tennessee on a friend's farm.

The ceremony, coordinated by Kelsey Rae Designs, was full of heartfelt familial touches, from an altar that Patrick built with her father, to a horseshoe made by her dad's foundry sitting above their heads for good luck, to a cake topper straight from her grandparents' wedding.

Patrick wore a Rue De Seine dress and custom Lucchese snakeskin boots while Tenpenny wore a dark green jacket paired with dark wash jeans and a cowboy hat.

Chase Bryant & Selena Weber

On Oct. 22, Country artist Chase Bryant wed model and entrepreneur Selena Weber in a hilltop ceremony in Round Mountain, Texas.

The couple was surrounded by friends and families at a venue overlooking 30-mile views of Texas Hill Country.

As she walked down the aisle, the bride wore a lace-accented Lillian West gown with a matching veil from Bridal House of Charleston, before changing into a classic sheath for the reception. Bryant complemented Weber's look with a white suit and bolero tie, as well as a pair of Chelsea boots.

Mary Lambert & Wyatt Paige Hermansen

After four years of dating, Mary Lambert and her partner Wyatt Paige Hermansen wed before 150 guests in Oakland, Maine on Oct. 22.

The "Same Love" singer and Hermansen, a professor who uses they/them pronouns, tied the knot in an outdoor ceremony at Enchanted Gables. The newlyweds then celebrated with a reception held in a barn on the grounds.

"We didn't want it to look like anybody else's wedding," Lambert told PEOPLE. "We really want it to be us. We wanted to be the wedding that people look at and say, 'I want to do that.'"

For the big day, Lambert wore a "sparkly" Essense of Australia dress that she picked out at Kleinfeld in New York City which was documented for an episode of the TLC series Say Yes to the Dress.

Hermansen got their suit from a Brooklyn-based company called Bindle & Keep, which specializes in custom-tailored suits for gender-nonconforming clients.

Lesley Murphy & Alex Kavanagh

Lauren Jenkins & Patrick Davis

Country artist Lauren Jenkins wed singer/songwriter Patrick Davis on Lookout Mountain in Georgia on Oct. 15.

The bride wore a stunning gown and veil designed by Grace Loves Lace as she exchanged handwritten vows with Davis.

"Since we are both songwriters, it makes sense that we would both write our own vows," she told PEOPLE with a laugh. "It's kind of like writing your own song instead of playing a cover."

Caleb Alexander Smith & Lindsay McCormick

NCIS star Caleb Alexander Smith and Sports Illustrated host Lindsay McCormick said "I do" on Oct. 14 at Château de Villette outside of Paris.

The bride wore a custom-fitted, turtleneck lace gown with an open back, pearl button accents, a long train and classic veil by Monique Lhuillier. Smith, opted for a bespoke midnight blue tuxedo by JB Clothiers.

"We wanted the wedding to be more of a family vacation and a chance for our siblings and parents to really bond than a big party," McCormick said, adding that the couple spread out their epic celebration over the span of a full week rather than have all the focus on 24 hours.

The couple added, "We really wanted our family to live in the moment, so we had a no phone policy for the 24 hours in the chateau."
